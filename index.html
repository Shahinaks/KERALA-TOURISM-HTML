TOP DESTINATIONS

Munnar is a scenic hill station nestled in the Western Ghats at an altitude of 1,600 meters. Known for its sprawling tea plantations, misty valleys, and pleasant climate, Munnar offers a perfect retreat for nature lovers. Key attractions include the Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, and the serene Mattupetty Dam. With its lush greenery, waterfalls, and winding trails, Munnar is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking tranquility and adventure.

Varkala is a charming coastal town known for its stunning cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea. The unique Varkala Beach, also called Papanasam Beach, is famous for its natural mineral springs and breathtaking sunsets. Visitors can enjoy activities like paragliding, surfing, and beach yoga. Varkala is also a spiritual hub, home to the ancient Janardanaswamy Temple and the serene Sivagiri Mutt. With its laid-back vibe, beachside cafes, and Ayurvedic spas, Varkala offers the perfect mix of relaxation and rejuvenation.

Kumarakom, a picturesque village on the banks of Vembanad Lake, is known for its serene backwaters, bird sanctuary, and luxury houseboats. It's a paradise for nature lovers and birdwatchers, offering glimpses of migratory birds like herons and kingfishers. Visitors can enjoy houseboat cruises, fishing, and scenic sunsets over the lake, making it a perfect spot for relaxation and honeymooners.

Wayanad, located in the Western Ghats, is known for its lush forests, mist-covered hills, and rich biodiversity. This serene district offers a mix of wildlife, history, and adventure. Key attractions include the Edakkal Caves with ancient petroglyphs, the Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary, and the scenic Meenmutty and Soochipara waterfalls. Wayanad is also a haven for trekkers, with popular trails leading to the Chembra Peak and the heart-shaped lake at its summit.

Often referred to as the Cultural Capital of Kerala, Thrissur is known for its grand festivals and heritage. The Thrissur Pooram festival, held at the Vadakkunnathan Temple, is one of the most spectacular temple festivals in India. The city also boasts attractions like the Athirappilly Waterfalls, Kerala Kalamandalam (a cultural institute), and the beautiful Guruvayur Temple, one of the stateâ€™s most famous pilgrimage sites.

ABOUT KERALA

Kerala has 14 districts, each offering unique experiences ranging from coastal charm to hill station serenity. The state enjoys a tropical monsoon climate, with its monsoon seasons being a major attraction. The culture of Kerala is deeply rooted in performing arts like Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Theyyam, along with martial arts like Kalaripayattu. Kerala is also famous for its spices, coconut-rich cuisine, and Ayurvedic healing practices.
